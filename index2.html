BANGA SOUP RECIPE

THE NIGER DELTA WAY!!!

About

Nigerian soups are the trademarks of the ethnic groups they originate from. Most Nigerians soups incorporate many vegetables and protein, and are usually finished off with palm oil or a stew/sauce. Today, Nigerians across communities enjoy all types of soups available. Here's a selection of six: Banga soup, Ofe Owerri, Efo riro, Okra soup, Ogbono soup and Edikainkong. Banga soup is made by extracting the juice from the seeds of raw palm kernel fruit. The seeds are cooked till they are tender and then pounded thoroughly until the juice is extracted and cooked. The Efik/Ibibios call it Abak Atama; the Yorubas call theirs Obe Eyin Ikpogiri.

Method of preparation

  1. Wash palm nuts, and place in a pot with water to boil
  2. After 30 minutes it should be thoroughly cooked so drain the water
  3. Place the banga in a bowl/pot/mortar and pound gently to extract the juice OR knead with your hands
  4. When the skin is peeled from the nuts, add warm water and sieve out the juice.
  5. Repeat the process 2 more times to ensure you extract all the juice.
  6. Place the extract from the palm nut or tin in a pot and bring to boil.
  7. Add banga spices (this spice brand we found was amazeballs!).
  8. Add periwinkle, smoked chicken, fresh catfish, salt, maggi, pepper...add everything and bring to boil.
  9. After about 20 minutes the soup should be cooked, thicker and with oil floating on top.
